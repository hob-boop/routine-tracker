02

House of Barber Non-Negotiables

Presence On time, set up, and ready before the first client. No exceptions.
Presentation Clean uniform, groomed appearance, station spotless between every client.
Consultation Every client gets a seated consult before scissors or clippers touch hair.
Finish Standard Every haircut signed off with a hot towel, razor line, and a mirror check.
Rebooking Offer a rebook at every visit. Retention is a technical skill, not a sales pitch.
Brand Voice How you speak about House of Barber online and offline is part of your standard.

Weekly Modules

Week 01

Brand Standards &
Shop Culture

Foundation — Who We Are & How We Show Up
Pillars Covered
Shop Brand Brand Standards

Week Objectives

  • Every team member can articulate the House of Barber brand story in under 60 seconds
  • Station set-up and presentation standard understood and self-assessed daily
  • Non-negotiables memorised and demonstrated without prompting
  • Uniform, hygiene, and punctuality standards embedded
  • Social media conduct and brand voice guidelines signed off
Monday
Day 1
Brand Story & Identity Session
  • Owner-led brand story walkthrough — origin, vision, values of House of Barber
  • Team signs and dates Brand Standards Agreement
  • First station set-up drill — timed, photographed as benchmark
  • Checkpoint: Each barber recites 3 core brand values without notes
Tuesday
Day 2
Presentation & Uniform Standards
  • Morning inspection drill — full uniform check before floor opens
  • Station cleanliness walkthrough: before, between, and after clients
  • Hygiene protocol review: tools, capes, hot towels, sanitisation order
  • Checkpoint: Pass/fail uniform inspection by end of shift
Wednesday
Day 3
The Client Journey — Start to Finish
  • Walk-through of the full client flow: arrival → greeting → consult → service → finish → rebook → exit
  • Role-play in pairs: one barber, one client. Swap roles after 20 mins
  • Identify and name each touchpoint where brand impression is made
  • Checkpoint: Each person maps the client journey from memory on paper
Thursday
Day 4
Social Media & Online Brand Conduct
  • Review House of Barber Instagram/TikTok/Facebook brand voice guide
  • What to post, what not to post — content do's and don'ts
  • How to tag, credit, and represent HOB across personal accounts
  • Checkpoint: Each barber submits one draft caption in HOB brand voice for review
Friday
Day 5
Competitor Awareness & HOB Positioning
  • Group discussion: What makes HOB different from other Christchurch barbershops?
  • Price point rationale — why we charge what we charge
  • How to handle "I can get it cheaper elsewhere" conversations naturally
  • Checkpoint: Pair up and practise value-positioning conversation (2 mins each)
Saturday
Day 6
Live Floor Application — Brand Standards On Busy Day
  • Owner or lead observes each barber's opening set-up and client greeting
  • Mid-shift debrief: one-on-one feedback on standards observed
  • Photo and video content capture (with client permission) — practice for Week 4
  • Checkpoint: Observation score card completed per barber (1–5 across 5 criteria)
Sunday
Day 7
Week 1 Review & Module Sign-Off
  • Group debrief: what landed, what needs reinforcement
  • Each barber self-rates on all Week 1 checkpoints (1–5 scale)
  • Owner countersigns Week 1 completion for each team member
  • Preview of Week 2: Technical Skills — what to prepare

Week 02

Technical Skills
Mastery

The Craft — Consistency, Precision, HOB Finish Standard
Pillars Covered
Technical Skills Brand Standards

Week Objectives

  • Every barber demonstrates the HOB fade standard to owner sign-off level
  • Consultation before every service is instinctive, not prompted
  • Razor line and hot towel finish are completed on every single client
  • Product knowledge tested — each barber can recommend 3 products with rationale
  • Speed-to-quality ratio tracked — target: full service under 45 mins at HOB standard
Monday
Day 8
Consultation Mastery — The HOB Consult Framework
  • The 5-question consult: lifestyle, maintenance, face shape, history, finish preference
  • Practice consults on each other — 10 mins each, observed and timed
  • How to redirect a client who doesn't know what they want
  • Checkpoint: Conduct 3 live consults during the day. Owner reviews notes taken
Tuesday
Day 9
Fade Fundamentals — HOB Fade Standard
  • Owner demos HOB signature fade on a model or mannequin
  • Step-by-step breakdown: guard order, blend zones, skin fade execution
  • Each barber performs a fade — video recorded for review
  • Checkpoint: Peer review using the HOB Fade Scorecard (distributed by owner)
Wednesday
Day 10
Scissor Work, Texture & Top Styling
  • Point cutting, slide cutting, and texturising techniques — when and why
  • Scissor over comb transition from fade top — consistency across head shape types
  • Styling products: pomade, clay, wax, paste — demo and application practice
  • Checkpoint: Each barber demonstrates a full scissor finish on a peer or model
Thursday
Day 11
Beard & Line-Up Standards
  • HOB beard shaping protocol: natural shape vs. sharp shape vs. skin fade beard
  • Razor work — straight razor safety, technique, and client communication
  • Line-up precision: temples, neckline, sideburns — the three zones
  • Checkpoint: Timed line-up on a model. Target: clean result in under 8 minutes
Friday
Day 12
Product Knowledge & Retail Recommendations
  • Full product range walkthrough: ingredient knowledge, hold levels, finish types
  • Matching product to hair type, lifestyle, and maintenance routine
  • Natural upsell language — "for your hair type, this would work best because…"
  • Checkpoint: Each barber passes a 10-question product knowledge quiz (≥ 8/10)
Saturday
Day 13
Full-Service Speed & Quality Drill on Live Clients
  • Every service timed from client seated to send-off. Target: ≤ 45 minutes
  • Owner or senior barber spot-checks consult, technique, and finish on 2 clients per barber
  • Video 1–2 services per barber (with permission) for review content and training archive
  • Checkpoint: Same-day feedback form completed per barber. Areas flagged before Sunday
Sunday
Day 14
Week 2 Technical Review & Video Debrief
  • Group replay of recorded fades and services — constructive group analysis
  • Each barber identifies their top skill improvement from this week
  • Any barber not meeting HOB fade standard receives a 1:1 plan before Week 3
  • Owner sign-off on Week 2 completion — note any conditional passes

Week 03

Customer Service
Excellence

Retention Is a Skill — Relationships, Rebooking & Revenue
Pillars Covered
Client Retention Client Acquisition

Week Objectives

  • Rebooking offered at the end of every single service — no exceptions
  • Each barber can handle at least 3 client complaint or concern scenarios confidently
  • Review request language embedded naturally into the client send-off
  • Upsell conversations feel like advice, not sales pitches
  • Phone and social media inquiry response standards understood and practised
Monday
Day 15
The Art of the Rebook — Making It Natural
  • Why rebooking is the single highest-value client retention action
  • HOB rebook script: timing, language, avoiding the hard-sell feel
  • Role-play: practise 3 different rebook conversations per barber
  • Checkpoint: Owner tests each barber with a "client who declines" scenario
Tuesday
Day 16
Handling Difficult Clients & Service Recovery
  • The 4-step complaint framework: Hear → Acknowledge → Solve → Retain
  • Scenario training: unhappy with cut, long wait, price objection, comparison to competitor
  • When to escalate to owner — clear boundary and language defined
  • Checkpoint: Each barber navigates 2 difficult scenarios with owner or senior observing
Wednesday
Day 17
Reviews, Referrals & Word-of-Mouth Activation
  • How and when to ask for a Google/Facebook review without being awkward
  • Referral language: "If you've got mates who need a cut, we'd love to have them in"
  • Understanding how a 5-star review impacts HOB's client acquisition
  • Checkpoint: Each barber scripts and practises their own natural review-request line
Thursday
Day 18
Upselling With Integrity — Products & Add-On Services
  • Difference between upselling and advising — how clients feel the difference
  • Identifying the natural moment to recommend a product or add-on during the service
  • The 3-step upsell: observe → connect to their problem → offer the solution
  • Checkpoint: Role-play upsell conversation. Observer scores naturalness 1–5
Friday
Day 19
Inquiry Handling — Phone, DMs, and Walk-Ins
  • HOB phone script: greeting, booking language, handling "how much?" questions
  • DM and social media inquiry response templates — tone, speed, close
  • Walk-in protocol: greeting, wait time communication, expectation management
  • Checkpoint: Timed DM response drill — team responds to 3 mock inquiries in under 5 mins
Saturday
Day 20
Full Customer Journey Live — Observe, Track, Refine
  • Owner tracks rebook rate across the day — target 70% or above
  • Monitor review requests: each barber asks at least 3 clients during the day
  • Note and flag any complaint or difficult interaction before it ends
  • Checkpoint: End-of-day rebook rate and review request tally submitted per barber
Sunday
Day 21
Week 3 Review — Numbers, Feedback & Sign-Off
  • Review Saturday's rebook rate and review request outcomes as a group
  • Each barber shares one service interaction they are proud of this week
  • Owner identifies any gaps going into Week 4 integration
  • Owner sign-off on Week 3 — customer service standards confirmed

Day 22
Consolidation — Identify and Close Gaps
  • Owner reviews all checkpoint outcomes from Weeks 1–3 and highlights any open items

Day 23
Content Creation Workshop — Your Brand, Your Camera
  • HOB content creation brief: what types of posts perform, what the brand needs

Day 24
Practice Assessment Day — Full Standard Observed
  • Owner observes minimum 2 full services per barber: consult, technique, finish, rebook
  • Assessment form used: Brand (W1), Technical (W2), Service (W3) all scored
  • Verbal feedback given after each assessment — no corrections during service
  • Checkpoint: Assessment form scores reviewed with each barber before end of day
Thursday
Day 25
One-on-One Development Conversations
  • 20-minute 1:1 between owner and each barber — strengths, gaps, next 90-day goals

Friday
Day 26
Content Review & Final Standard Polish

Saturday
Day 27

  • All barbers on full independent standard — no prompting, no reminders

  • Rebook rate and review request rate tracked for the day

Sunday
Day 28
Graduation — Curriculum Completion & Celebration
